What's Included

Full-scope land clearing.

From a one-acre residential pad to a forty-acre commercial tract, we handle the whole sequence under one accountable team — including the regulatory side most clearing contractors leave to you.

01

Brush & Forestry Mulching

Track-mounted forestry mulchers grind standing brush and small timber in place into a ground-cover layer — no burn piles, no haul-off, minimal topsoil disturbance.

02

Tree Removal & Grubbing

Selective tree cutting where you want to preserve specific oaks or pines, full clear-cut for pad sites, plus root-ball grubbing so the next contractor isn't fighting buried stumps.

03

Stump Grinding & Haul-Off

Stump grinding below grade, debris haul-off to permitted facilities, and on-site burn coordination where allowed under county rules.

04

Erosion Control & SWPPP

Silt fence, stabilized construction entrances, inlet protection, and the TCEQ Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan paperwork required on any disturbance of one acre or more.

How It Works

From walkthrough to cleared site.

1

Site Walk & Survey Review

We meet you on the property, walk the boundaries against the survey, and identify trees to save, drainage features to preserve, and access points for equipment.

2

Method & Bid

Written line-item bid that picks the right method per zone — mulching here, cut-and-haul there, selective save around drainage. Permits and SWPPP filed where required.

3

Clear & Stabilize

Crews mobilize with the right equipment for the scope, install erosion control first, then clear in a sequence that keeps the site stable and on-grade for what comes next.

4

Hand-off to Grading

If we're doing your grading too, the equipment stays on site. If you're using a separate civil contractor, we coordinate the handoff so they can roll straight into pad work.
