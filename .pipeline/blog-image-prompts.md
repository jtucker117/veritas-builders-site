# Veritas Builders blog — image generation prompts

One prompt per post in the queue. Tuned for ChatGPT's image generator
(DALL-E 3) but works equally well with Midjourney, Imagen, or Flux.

## How to use

1. Copy the **Master style block** below.
2. Append the per-post prompt for the slug you're generating.
3. Paste into ChatGPT (or your generator of choice) with: *"Generate a
   16:9 landscape image, 1600×900 if possible."*
4. Save the resulting image as `<slug>.jpg` and drop it in
   `images/projects/`. The existing blog template will pick it up
   from `https://veritasbuilderstx.com/images/projects/<slug>.jpg`.
5. Optimize before committing — sips quality 70, max 1600px wide
   (see prior recompress pass).

## Master style block (prepend to every prompt below)

> Photorealistic documentary-style photograph. Texas / Greater Houston
> setting (humid Gulf Coast, big sky, pine forests inland, oak trees,
> warm natural daylight). Professional construction context. Real
> materials, real dust where appropriate, slight depth of field, shot
> at roughly f/8. Warm neutral color grading. **No text, no logos, no
> watermarks, no signage with words. No visible faces (people only
> from behind, in silhouette, or out of frame).** 16:9 landscape
> aspect ratio (1600×900 target).

## Tips for getting better images out of DALL-E

1. **Always say "no text, no logos, no watermarks"** — DALL-E will try to
   put garbled fake signage on jobsites otherwise.
2. **Specify lighting** — "warm late-afternoon sun" / "soft overcast
   daylight" / "bright midday" — DALL-E defaults to dramatic golden
   hour everywhere, which gets repetitive.
3. **Specify aspect ratio in the prompt itself**, not just in the UI:
   *"16:9 landscape composition, wide horizontal frame"*.
4. **Faces are risky** — say *"no visible faces, people only from behind
   or in silhouette"* to avoid uncanny portraits.
5. **Regenerate variations** — DALL-E often nails the third or fourth
   attempt better than the first.
6. **Save the raw at the highest available resolution** (DALL-E 3
   currently outputs 1792×1024 for landscape), then run it through
   the same `sips` recompress as the other blog images so it ends up
   under 400KB at 1600px wide.
